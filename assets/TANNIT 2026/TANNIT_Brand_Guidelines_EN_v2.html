TANNIT Agency 01 · Introduction 04
About TANNIT

Who we are

TANNIT Agency is a full-service pharmaceutical direct marketing execution agency. We coordinate design, production, database management and logistics under a single point of contact, eliminating multi-vendor complexity for pharmaceutical companies and the agencies that serve them.

Our model is built on operational precision. From briefing to documented delivery to the field representative, every project is handled with traceability, accuracy and zero operational burden on the client.

«The simpler, the better.»

TANNIT Core Principle

This principle governs every design decision. When in doubt between adding or removing an element: remove it. Clarity and visual cleanliness take priority over any decorative effect.

About this document

This Brand Guidelines defines TANNIT's visual identity rules. Its purpose is to enable anyone working on design, communications or branded materials to make coherent decisions independently. The guidelines here are rules, not suggestions.

Who it is for

  • Internal design team
  • External design partners and agencies
  • Web and development teams
  • Print production suppliers

What it covers

  • Logo system and usage rules
  • Colour palette and specifications
  • Typography and type hierarchy
  • Digital and print applications
  • Brand voice and tone
Exceptions: Any design decision not covered by this document must be approved before implementation. Contact: hello@tannitagency.es

TANNIT Agency 02 · Brand Strategy 07
Context

Pharmaceutical Sector

TANNIT works exclusively with pharmaceutical companies and agencies in this sector. This context drives design decisions that would be unnecessary in other industries.

Pharmaceutical sector: The design must convey trust, professionalism and credibility. Nothing that looks like a "creative agency" or "startup" aesthetic.

B2B audience: Target audience includes marketing managers, medical affairs teams and procurement departments. They expect sophistication and seriousness, not freshness or disruption.

Execution, not creativity: TANNIT competes on getting things done right, on time, with traceability. Design reflects that: clean, ordered, no unnecessary ornamentation.

Target Audience

ProfileWhat they valueDesign implication
Marketing manager Reliability, deadline compliance Show process, timeline, traceability
Medical affairs Material accuracy, regulatory compliance Professional tone, verifiable data
Procurement Single vendor, clear costs, documentation Emphasise "all under one roof"
Agencies White-label, hassle-free execution Show success without overexposing client

Design Implications

Apply

Prioritise process, workflow and traceability.

Expertise shown through clean execution.

Data, figures and facts over emotional appeals.

Photography of campaigns actually produced by TANNIT.

Avoid

Creative agency aesthetics or trendy design movements.

Vague claims or generic "partnership" language.

Stock photography or staged, generic imagery.

Visual effects for decoration alone.

Primary Colours

Brand Colours

TANNIT's palette is built on two primary colours supported by a range of neutral tones. Use primary colours purposefully. Every colour application must have a reason.

#8A6E9A
Violet · Primary
HEX #8A6E9A RGB 138 · 110 · 154 CMYK 10 · 29 · 0 · 40
CTAs, headings, navigation, key brand moments. Conveys trust and professionalism in the pharmaceutical context.
#6AA8A4
Teal · Secondary
HEX #6AA8A4 RGB 106 · 168 · 164 CMYK 37 · 0 · 2 · 34
Accents, section labels, secondary CTAs, highlights. Adds approachability and freshness to complement the Violet.

Colour Usage Rules

Apply

Violet leads; Teal supports. Maintain this hierarchy.

Maximum two primary colours per section or layout area.

Use opacity variations (40%, 67%, 82%) to create depth.

Soft gradients between brand tones for hero areas.

Avoid

Bright or saturated colours outside the defined palette.

Violet and Teal at full opacity on the same element.

High-contrast multicolour gradients.

Multiple colours competing within the same view.

Permitted Gradients

Gradients are part of TANNIT's visual language for backgrounds and hero sections. All gradient transitions must use brand colours exclusively.

Violet Light → Warm
Teal (low opacity) → Warm
Violet → Violet Light

Rules

Apply

Montserrat for headings and titles only.

Helvetica for all body copy, descriptions and data.

One H1 per page. Clear visual hierarchy at all times.

Labels always uppercase with 0.12–0.16em letter spacing.

Avoid

Additional font families without explicit approval.

Montserrat for paragraphs or long-form text.

All caps on text longer than three words.

Mixing weights inconsistently within the same block.

Grid System

Layout Principles

12-Column Grid

All web layouts use a 12-column grid with a maximum content width of 1200 px. Columns have 24 px gutters.

Margin zone (cols 1–2, 11–12)  ·  Content area (cols 3–10)  ·  Max width 1200 px · 24 px gutters

Spacing Scale (8 px base)

All spacing (padding, margin, gap) uses multiples of 8 px. Never use odd values.

White Space Principle

Apply

Generous space between sections. When in doubt, add more.

One primary visual focus per section.

Progressive disclosure: don't show everything at once.

Mobile-first: design for the smallest breakpoint first.

Avoid

Crowded sections where elements compete for attention.

Multi-column layouts where a single column works better.

Decorative elements that take up space without function.

Animations that distract from reading the content.

Voice

TANNIT Agency Brand Guidelines v2.0
TANNIT Agency 10 · Brand Voice & Tone 25
Positioning Axis

Precision as a form of care

TANNIT's voice is not "warm but professional." That is a non-decision. TANNIT's voice is built on a specific insight: specificity is the warmth. "Delivered in 27 days" is more reassuring than "fast delivery." "One contact" is more human than "personalised service." Precision and simplicity are the same move: we don't add warmth on top of the operational language; the operational language, done precisely, is the warmth.

«Give us the brief. We've done this before.»

The implicit promise behind every TANNIT communication

Who we are speaking to

TANNIT's primary audience is the account director or production manager at a creative agency , the person responsible for delivering a pharma campaign who needs a reliable execution partner behind them. Their anxiety is not regulatory; it is reputational. They need TANNIT to make them look good in front of their client, execute without being babied, and solve problems before they become visible.

The voice must answer one question above all others: "Will this partner make my life easier or harder?" Every sentence we write should make the answer obvious.

Three voice registers

01 · The one who has done it before

TANNIT does not say "we can handle this." It says "we've handled exactly this." There are no conditionals, no hedging, no future promises. The tense is past or present: we deliver, we coordinate, we document. Experience is shown through specificity, never claimed through adjectives.

02 · Direct without being dry

Clarity is not coldness. We remove corporate bloat, not to sound friendly, but because every unnecessary word signals uncertainty. Short sentences with an active subject. Data before description. The full stop instead of the comma followed by a qualification. If a sentence can be cut in half and still mean the same thing, cut it.

03 · No ego about the role

TANNIT exists to make the agency's work shine. We do not compete for credit, do not explain how hard the process was, do not ask to be acknowledged. When something goes sideways, we surface it first and arrive with a solution. The voice never dramatises operational complexity. It makes complexity disappear.

On women-led leadership: a note on application

This is not a claim in the voice. It is a behavioural standard. TANNIT listens before proposing. Does not become defensive when a brief changes. Acknowledges a mistake before the client finds it. Is direct without aggression. These behaviours are the leadership. The label is optional.

Application

Vocabulary & Tone in Context

Word choices: use / avoid

UseAvoidWhy it matters
We manage / deliver / coordinate We offer solutions / We provide Active verb signals ownership. "Offer" signals intention, not commitment.
In X days / X countries / X pieces Fast / efficient / agile A number cannot be disputed. An adjective can always be questioned.
One contact. One process. Comprehensive end-to-end solutions Concrete removes the need to explain. Jargon requires unpacking.
Documented traceability Full transparency / total visibility Operational language builds credibility in regulated sectors. Abstract claims do not.
Your agency / your client / your campaign The sector / stakeholders / end users Naming the real person signals we understand the actual relationship.
If something changes, you hear from us first We are committed to communication A behaviour described is more credible than a value declared.

Tone in context

SituationRegisterExample
First contact with agency Confident, brief "We handle design, production and delivery for pharma campaigns. One contact, full traceability. Want to see what we've done?"
Proposal Data-first, no preamble "40 pieces. 5 countries. 27 days. 100% EMA compliance. This is what the last campaign looked like."
Project update email Precise, proactive "Production is on track. Delivery confirmed for Thursday. If anything shifts before then, you'll know before your client does."
Problem surfaces mid-project Direct, solution-led "There's a two-day delay on one SKU. We've already rerouted logistics. New delivery window: Monday. No impact on total timeline."
Social media / LinkedIn Grounded, no superlatives "500 personalised folders. 98% confirmed deliveries. 19 days. Neurology launch, done."

Writing examples

Write like this

"Design, production, personalisation, warehousing, delivery. One contract, one contact, documented at every stage. You focus on the campaign. We handle what comes after the brief."

Not like this

"We offer a comprehensive, end-to-end marketing execution service designed to empower agencies and pharmaceutical companies to achieve their communication goals efficiently."

Write like this

"If a deadline moves, you hear from us first, not last. We surface problems early and arrive with a solution, not an explanation."

Not like this

"At TANNIT, we are committed to transparent, proactive communication and we always strive to keep our valued partners informed throughout the process."

Write like this

"We've done this in regulated sectors. Give us the brief. We know what to expect and we know what to watch for."

Not like this

"Our experienced team is passionate about navigating the unique challenges of the pharmaceutical marketing landscape with creativity and expertise."
